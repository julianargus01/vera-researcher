# VERA — Verified Evidence Research Analyst

VERA is a research partner for cancer patients evaluating complementary and alternative therapies alongside conventional treatment. Her tagline is the problem statement: your doctor has 12 minutes, the internet has an agenda, and most patients are left to navigate a flood of noise alone. VERA closes that gap — not by summarizing what's out there, but by doing the investigative work a trained researcher would do: retrieving primary sources, weighing the evidence hierarchy, flagging conflicts of interest, and naming what's genuinely unknown. The target user is the informed cancer patient or their caregiver — someone who has already decided to ask hard questions and needs a tool that can hold up its end of the conversation.

VERA's methodology is a three-phase cascade enforced by the file system itself, not by hopeful instruction. Phase 1 is a progressive scoping dialogue — one question per turn to establish cancer type and stage, current treatment, target outcome, and contraindication context, ending with a `SCOPING-SUMMARY` artifact written to disk. Phase 2 is systematic evidence retrieval — a 6-source fallback chain (Europe PMC → PubMed → ClinicalTrials.gov → Semantic Scholar → Unpaywall → WebSearch), with every source tagged by a 6-tier CAM evidence hierarchy (T1 RCTs and meta-analyses through T6 anecdote) and an inline conflict-of-interest flag — ending with an `evidence-ledger.md` artifact. Phase 3 is two-audience delivery — a patient-facing evaluation with the verdict in the first two or three sentences, tier tags and COI flags preserved in plain language, plus a one-page oncologist handoff — written to `final-report.md`. Phase 2 cannot begin until Phase 1's artifact exists; Phase 3 cannot begin until Phase 2's exists. The cascade is structural, not aspirational.

The architecture is Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM) implemented to canonical, with one extension earned through empirical failure: **phase isolation via subprocess dispatch**. `identity.md` is a tiny L1 routing table; each phase's `rules.md` is the Pattern 1 contract (Inputs / Process / Outputs); L3 references and per-phase voice files load only when their phase routes there. Every behavioral file is 80% about the work and 20% about VERA (Mistake 4). The design extension: at end of Phase 1, the main agent spawns Phase 2 and Phase 3 each as separate `claude -p` subprocesses with fresh context windows. This was forced by testing — same-turn cascading bloated context past Haiku's compaction threshold, the model lost its rules mid-cascade, and deliverables broke. Subprocess isolation eliminated the bloat without violating ICM's "routing table is the only logic" principle (each subprocess just re-evaluates the routing table on fresh file state). The build was end-to-end simulated across multiple Haiku cascades on real patient scenarios (mistletoe + HER2+ breast, fasting + R-CHOP NHL, turmeric + FOLFIRINOX pancreatic, cannabis oil + colorectal); each cascade produced a clean 6-page PDF with URL-encoded chat-link delivery. The deployment case is straightforward: integrative oncology clinics, patient advocacy organizations, cancer support networks, and health system patient education programs all face the same problem — patients arriving with questions sourced from testimonial sites and supplement company blogs, with no one to do a proper literature review with them. VERA is that review, packaged as a research analyst with a consistent methodology, structurally enforced refusals, and a delivery format that respects two audiences. She is not a medical device. She is the prepared patient's researcher — and that is a product category that does not yet exist at this quality.
