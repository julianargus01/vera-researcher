# CAM Categories

7-category oncology taxonomy (NCCIH-adapted). Classify before applying evidence-tiers.md and coi-flags.md.

---

## 1. Biological / Natural Products

**Definition:** Natural-source substances regulated as dietary supplements (DSHEA), not drugs.

**Examples:** Turmeric, mistletoe, IV vitamin C, melatonin, cannabis/CBD, probiotics, astragalus.

**Evidence:** Most-studied. Ranges from Cochrane-reviewed (mistletoe, vitamin C) to anecdotal. Most RCTs small and industry-funded.

**Key risks:** CYP450 drug interactions (St. John's Wort, grapefruit); antioxidants may interfere with oxidative therapies; probiotics contraindicated in severe immunosuppression.

---

## 2. Mind-Body Practices

**Definition:** Techniques using psychological mechanisms to affect physiological function.

**Examples:** MBSR, guided imagery, hypnosis, biofeedback, yoga, music therapy.

**Evidence:** Moderate for symptom management (anxiety, pain, nausea). No anti-tumor evidence.

**Key risks:** Very low. Requires trained practitioners to avoid harmful framing.

---

## 3. Physical / Manipulative

**Definition:** Therapies involving physical contact, movement, or manipulation.

**Examples:** Acupuncture, massage, chiropractic, reflexology, osteopathic manipulation.

**Evidence:** Moderate for acupuncture (nausea, pain) and massage. Chiropractic evidence-poor in oncology.

**Key risks:** Contraindicated over tumor sites, thrombocytopenia, lymphedema, bone metastases. Chiropractic contraindicated with spinal metastases.

---

## 4. Energy-Based

**Definition:** Therapies premised on biofield manipulation or electromagnetic/thermal energy.

**Examples:** Reiki, therapeutic touch, PEMF therapy, infrared sauna, low-level laser therapy.

**Evidence:** Poor. No reproducible biofield evidence; small RCTs for PEMF/LLLT only.

**Key risks:** Rife machines and similar devices — no evidence, fraud risk. Infrared sauna contraindicated in active chemo.

---

## 5. Whole Medical Systems

**Definition:** Complete systems of theory and practice.

**Examples:** TCM, Ayurveda, Homeopathy, Naturopathy.

**Evidence:** Highly variable. TCM acupuncture strongest. Homeopathy: Cochrane finds no evidence beyond placebo. TCM herbal research subject to geographic capture (Chinese government funding).

**Key risks:** Ayurvedic heavy metal preparations directly toxic. TCM herbs: aristolochic acid (nephrotoxic, carcinogenic). Homeopathy: treatment delay risk.

---

## 6. Dietary Interventions

**Definition:** Structured dietary regimens promoted for cancer prevention, treatment, or recovery.

**Examples:** Ketogenic diet, fasting, juice cleanses, Gerson therapy, alkaline diet.

**Evidence:** Poor to emerging. Keto/fasting has small trials; Gerson has none; alkaline diet premise physiologically implausible.

**Key risks:** Gerson coffee enemas — electrolyte imbalances, bowel perforation, deaths reported. Grapefruit: CYP3A4 inhibitor. Extreme restriction causes muscle wasting.

---

## 7. Spiritual / Mind-Body-Spirit

**Definition:** Practices incorporating spiritual or religious belief as a therapeutic mechanism.

**Examples:** Intercessory prayer, spiritual healing, crystal therapy, shamanic practices.

**Evidence:** Very poor for treatment efficacy; some QoL data for prayer as coping.

**Key risks:** Treatment delay or refusal of conventional care.
