# Evidence Tiers

6-tier CAM-specific hierarchy. Tier 1 and Tier 2 are kept separate — individual industry-funded RCTs routinely contradict Cochrane pooled analyses.

---

## Tier 1 — Cochrane Reviews & High-Quality Meta-Analyses of RCTs

**Examples:** Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews; GRADE-rated meta-analyses from pre-registered protocols.

**Weight:** Highest. Pre-registered protocol, unpublished source search, updated on new evidence. Report GRADE certainty with every citation.

**Downgrade when:** GRADE certainty Low or Very Low; review >5 years in active research area; search methods undescribed.

**COI flag:** Flag if funded by a commercial entity with financial interest in the outcome.

---

## Tier 2 — Individual RCTs (Large, Multi-Center, Double-Blinded)

**Examples:** Phase III trials, n ≥ 200, multi-site, double-blinded.

**Weight:** Strong directional evidence. Single trials cannot account for cross-study publication bias.

**Downgrade when:** Single site; n < 50; open-label; high dropout (>20%); industry-funded.

**COI flag:** Name funding source on every citation; flag sponsor involvement in data analysis or publication.

---

## Tier 3 — Observational Studies (Cohort, Case-Control, Cross-Sectional)

**Examples:** Cohort studies of supplement users; case-control studies of CAM use in a cancer population.

**Weight:** Directional signal. Cannot establish causation; confounding is the major limitation.

**Downgrade when:** No control group; retrospective; self-reported exposure; no confounder adjustment; n < 100.

**COI flag:** Flag funding source; flag if sponsor produces or distributes the intervention.

---

## Tier 4 — Case Reports, Case Series, Pilot Studies

**Examples:** Reports of 1–20 patients; feasibility or tolerability trials with no control arm.

**Weight:** Hypothesis-generating only. Cannot establish efficacy.

**Downgrade when:** Single case; no stated follow-up; outcome not independently verified.

**COI flag:** Flag if authored or sponsored by product manufacturer.

---

## Tier 5 — Grey Literature (Preprints, Institutional Guidelines, Conference Abstracts)

**Examples:** bioRxiv/medRxiv preprints; NIH/NCCIH position statements; conference abstracts; DSHEA-context regulatory documents.

**Weight:** Unvalidated. Mark as such in every citation.

**DSHEA note:** US supplements require no pre-market safety or efficacy proof. Apply DSHEA flag to any supplement citation.

**COI flag:** Flag industry affiliation on all grey literature authors and sponsors.

---

## Tier 6 — Anecdotal (Patient Forums, Testimonials, Practitioner Reports, Social Media)

**Examples:** Reddit cancer forums; Facebook patient groups; unverified practitioner case reports; YouTube testimonials.

**Weight:** Signal only — not citable as evidence. Use to identify patterns worth investigating.

**COI flag:** Note platform and reporter's commercial affiliations.
